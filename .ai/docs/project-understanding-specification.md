# Project Understanding Specification

The `project-understanding.md` artifact is the central engineering reference generated by the `Project Understanding Engine`. It synthesizes all human-provided `Project Sources` (and `Project Context` if available) into a single, cohesive document optimized for AI reasoning.

## Location
`.ai/context/project-understanding.md`

## Ownership
- **Owner**: AI (Project Understanding Engine)
- **Source of Truth**: The human's `Project Sources` and `Project Context`.
- **Note**: The AI must never silently rewrite human intent. If conflicts exist in the sources, this document highlights them rather than silently overriding them.

## Structure

The artifact should follow this suggested structure:

### Executive Summary
A concise overview of what the project is, why it exists, and its primary value proposition.

### Purpose & Vision
- **Purpose**: The core problem the project solves.
- **Vision**: The long-term goal or desired state of the project.

### Project Scope & Success Criteria
- **Scope**: What is included and explicitly excluded from the project.
- **Success Criteria**: Measurable or verifiable outcomes that define project success.

### Target Users
Who the project is built for and their key needs.

### Functional Requirements
A synthesized list of what the system must do, extracted from requirements documents, stories, or specifications.

### Non-functional Requirements
Performance, security, scalability, accessibility, and other quality attributes.

### Architecture Overview
High-level system design, reflecting diagrams (Draw.io, PlantUML) or architectural docs.

### Major Components
Key modules, services, or packages and their responsibilities.

### Domain Terminology
A glossary of project-specific terms to ensure consistent language across the workspace.

### Technology Stack
Languages, frameworks, databases, and tools used.

### Constraints & Assumptions
- **Constraints**: Hard limits (e.g., budget, specific technology mandates, deadlines).
- **Assumptions**: Presumed truths that impact engineering decisions.

### Engineering Goals & Learning Opportunities
Technical aspirations and areas where the team/AI aims to improve or innovate.

### Known Unknowns & Open Questions
Areas where the `Project Sources` lack clarity, contradict each other, or are incomplete.

### References
Links to the original `Project Sources` (e.g., `README.md`, `requirements.pdf`, `architecture.drawio`) used to generate this understanding.

## Guidelines for Generation
- **Be Concise**: Extract only what is necessary for engineering reasoning.
- **Do Not Duplicate**: Do not copy-paste entire source documents; synthesize them.
- **Conflict Highlighting**: If `Project Context` says X but a PDF says Y, document the discrepancy under Open Questions.
